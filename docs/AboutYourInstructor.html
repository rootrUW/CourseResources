About Your Instructor

Welcome to the Course!

Hello, I am Randal Root, and I will be your instructor for this course. I am a senior consultant and technical educator specializing in SQL Server business intelligence (BI) solutions, ETL automation, database administration, and data-focused application development. I am excited to work with you as you build your skills in programming, databases, and data analytics.

Teaching Approach

My teaching philosophy is based on the idea that every student can learn complex technical skills when they are given clear structure, authentic practice, and a supportive environment. I aim to:

  • Break complex topics into manageable, step-by-step lessons
  • Provide real-world, project-based assignments that build practical skills
  • Emphasize professional standards in code, documentation, and tooling
  • Create an inclusive, accessible learning environment where questions are welcome
  • Encourage a growth mindset and treat errors as a normal part of learning

In this course, you will be coached, not judged. My goal is to help you think like an engineer and feel confident working with modern tools and technologies.

I also continue to consult so that what I teach reflects current industry tools, challenges, and expectations.

As a technical instructor at the University of Washington, I teach a range of applied technology subjects, including:

  • SQL and database fundamentals
  • Python and C# programming
  • ASP.NET web development
  • Business Intelligence and data analytics

I design and deliver course materials such as videos, demonstrations, labs, quizzes, and written guides to support both your conceptual understanding and your ability to apply these skills in real-world scenarios.

How This Helps You as a Student

Because I continue to work in the field as a consultant and developer, the examples and projects in this course are drawn from real-world scenarios. You will:

  • Practice using tools that are common in industry settings
  • Learn how to design, build, and troubleshoot systems that resemble real BI and data solutions
  • Develop habits such as documentation, version control, testing, and professional communication
  • Gain experience that you can discuss in job interviews or apply directly at work

I know that learning new technologies can feel challenging, especially if you are balancing work, family, and school. My goal is to provide a structured, supportive environment where you can make steady progress. I will ask you to practice, experiment, and sometimes struggle a bit with the material, but you will not be alone in that process.

If you have questions, please ask. If you get stuck, reach out. I am looking forward to working with you and helping you build skills that will serve you well in your career.
